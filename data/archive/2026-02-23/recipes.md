## Lauren's Chili
*Hearty sweet potato chili with ground beef, kidney beans, and mushrooms — served with fresh cornbread*
**Prep time:** 15 min | **Cook time:** 45 min | **Total:** 60 min
**Serves:** 5 (+ 2 days leftovers) | **Healthiness:** 8/10 | **Deliciousness:** 9/10 | **Cost:** ~$16

### Ingredients
- [ ] 2.5 lbs ground beef
- [ ] 4 sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed (about 1-inch pieces)
- [ ] 1 pack (8 oz) mushrooms, sliced
- [ ] 2 onions, chopped
- [ ] 4 cans (15 oz each) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
- [ ] 4 cans (15 oz each) tomato sauce
- [ ] 2 tablespoons olive oil
- [ ] 2 tablespoons chili powder
- [ ] 1 tablespoon cumin
- [ ] 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- [ ] Salt and pepper to taste
- [ ] 1 box cornbread mix (+ ingredients per box: egg, milk)

### Instructions

Brown the Beef:
- [ ] Heat **2 tablespoons olive oil** in the Le Creuset over medium-high heat.
- [ ] Add **2.5 lbs ground beef** and break apart with a spoon.
- [ ] Cook until browned, about 8 minutes. Drain excess fat if needed.

Sauté the Aromatics:
- [ ] Add **2 chopped onions** and **8 oz sliced mushrooms** to the pot.
- [ ] Cook until onions are soft and mushrooms have released their liquid, about 5 minutes.
- [ ] Stir in **2 tablespoons chili powder**, **1 tablespoon cumin**, and **1 teaspoon smoked paprika**. Toast for 1 minute.

Build the Chili:
- [ ] Add **4 cubed sweet potatoes** to the pot and stir to coat in spices.
- [ ] Pour in **4 cans tomato sauce** and **4 cans kidney beans** (drained and rinsed).
- [ ] Stir everything together and bring to a boil.
- [ ] Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30-35 minutes until sweet potatoes are fork-tender.
- [ ] Season with **salt and pepper** to taste.

Make the Cornbread:
- [ ] While the chili simmers, prepare **1 box cornbread mix** according to package directions.
- [ ] Bake in the oven while the chili finishes cooking.

### Leftover Ideas
- Day 2: Chili-topped baked potatoes (use the $1.49 Idaho potatoes from Cermak)
- Day 3: Chili mac — toss leftover chili with cooked elbow macaroni and top with shredded cheese

## Taco Tuesday — Al Pastor Style
*Seasoned al pastor pork with fresh toppings, served in warm tortillas with beans and rice*
**Prep time:** 10 min | **Cook time:** 20 min | **Total:** 30 min
**Serves:** 5 (+ 1 day leftovers) | **Healthiness:** 7/10 | **Deliciousness:** 9/10 | **Cost:** ~$14

### Ingredients
- [ ] 2 lbs pork taco meat from freezer
- [ ] 1 lb marinated pork taco meat (al pastor) from Cermak
- [ ] 1 can (15 oz) black beans
- [ ] 1.5 cups long-grain rice
- [ ] 3 cups water or chicken broth (for rice)
- [ ] 15 small flour or corn tortillas
- [ ] 1 avocado, sliced (use the ripe one on counter)
- [ ] 3 limes, cut into wedges
- [ ] 1/2 bunch cilantro, chopped
- [ ] 1 white onion, diced
- [ ] 1 cup salsa (La Costena or from pantry)
- [ ] Sour cream for serving
- [ ] 1 cup shredded cheese (Mexican blend)

### Instructions

Start the Rice:
- [ ] Combine **1.5 cups long-grain rice** and **3 cups water or broth** in a pot.
- [ ] Bring to a boil, reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for 18 minutes. Let sit covered 5 minutes, then fluff.

Cook the Taco Meat:
- [ ] Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat.
- [ ] Add **2 lbs pork taco meat from freezer** (thaw first or cook from partially frozen, breaking apart as it heats).
- [ ] Add **1 lb Cermak al pastor meat** alongside.
- [ ] Cook until browned and heated through, about 12-15 minutes, breaking into small pieces.

Warm the Beans & Tortillas:
- [ ] Heat **1 can black beans** (drained) in a small saucepan with a splash of water and a pinch of cumin.
- [ ] Warm **15 tortillas** in a dry skillet or wrapped in foil in a 300°F oven.

Prep the Toppings:
- [ ] Dice **1 white onion**, chop **cilantro**, slice **1 avocado**, and cut **3 limes** into wedges.
- [ ] Set out **salsa**, **sour cream**, and **shredded cheese** in bowls.

### Leftover Ideas
- Day 2: Taco bowls — leftover meat over rice with beans, cheese, salsa, and sour cream. Skip the tortillas.

## Sheet Pan Chicken Thighs with Green Beans & Roasted Potatoes
*Crispy-skinned chicken thighs roasted alongside green beans and potato wedges with garlic and herbs*
**Prep time:** 15 min | **Cook time:** 25 min | **Total:** 40 min
**Serves:** 5 | **Healthiness:** 8/10 | **Deliciousness:** 8/10 | **Cost:** ~$10

### Ingredients
- [ ] 2.5 lbs boneless chicken thighs (from freezer, thawed)
- [ ] 1.5 lbs green beans, ends trimmed
- [ ] 1.5 lbs Idaho potatoes, cut into wedges (from Cermak 5 lb bag)
- [ ] 3 tablespoons olive oil
- [ ] 4 cloves garlic, minced
- [ ] 1 teaspoon paprika
- [ ] 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- [ ] 1 teaspoon dried Italian herbs (oregano, thyme, or Italian seasoning)
- [ ] Salt and pepper to taste
- [ ] 1 lemon, cut into wedges (for serving)

### Instructions

Preheat & Prep:
- [ ] Preheat oven to 425°F.
- [ ] Cut **1.5 lbs Idaho potatoes** into wedges and place on a large sheet pan.
- [ ] Toss potatoes with **1 tablespoon olive oil**, **salt**, and **pepper**. Spread in a single layer.
- [ ] Roast potatoes alone for 10 minutes to give them a head start.

Season the Chicken:
- [ ] Pat **2.5 lbs chicken thighs** dry with paper towels.
- [ ] Toss with **1 tablespoon olive oil**, **1 teaspoon paprika**, **1 teaspoon garlic powder**, **1 teaspoon Italian herbs**, **salt**, and **pepper**.

Prep the Green Beans:
- [ ] Toss **1.5 lbs trimmed green beans** with **1 tablespoon olive oil**, **4 cloves minced garlic**, **salt**, and **pepper**.

Roast Everything:
- [ ] After the 10-minute potato head start, add the seasoned chicken thighs and green beans to the sheet pan.
- [ ] Arrange so chicken is in the center and vegetables are around the edges.
- [ ] Roast for 20-25 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F internal temperature and green beans are tender-crisp.
- [ ] Serve with **lemon wedges** for squeezing.

### Leftover Ideas
- If extra: Shred leftover chicken for quesadillas or chicken salad wraps with the romaine lettuce from the fridge.

## Sheet Pan Pork Chops with Roasted Veggies
*Juicy center-cut pork chops with roasted potatoes, green beans, and onions — simple and satisfying*
**Prep time:** 10 min | **Cook time:** 25 min | **Total:** 35 min
**Serves:** 5 | **Healthiness:** 8/10 | **Deliciousness:** 8/10 | **Cost:** ~$12

### Ingredients
- [ ] 5 center-cut pork chops (about 2.5 lbs total, from Cermak at $2.29/lb)
- [ ] 1.5 lbs Idaho potatoes, cut into 1-inch cubes (from Cermak 5 lb bag)
- [ ] 1 lb green beans, ends trimmed
- [ ] 1 large white onion, cut into wedges
- [ ] 3 tablespoons olive oil
- [ ] 2 teaspoons garlic powder
- [ ] 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- [ ] 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- [ ] Salt and pepper to taste

### Instructions

Preheat & Prep Veggies:
- [ ] Preheat oven to 425°F.
- [ ] Cut **1.5 lbs Idaho potatoes** into 1-inch cubes and **1 large white onion** into wedges.
- [ ] Toss potatoes and onions with **1.5 tablespoons olive oil**, **1 teaspoon garlic powder**, **salt**, and **pepper**.
- [ ] Spread on a sheet pan and roast for 10 minutes.

Season the Pork Chops:
- [ ] Pat **5 center-cut pork chops** dry.
- [ ] Rub with **1.5 tablespoons olive oil**, **1 teaspoon garlic powder**, **1 teaspoon smoked paprika**, **1 teaspoon dried thyme**, **salt**, and **pepper**.

Roast Together:
- [ ] After the 10-minute veggie head start, add **1 lb trimmed green beans** and the seasoned pork chops to the pan.
- [ ] Roast for 20-22 minutes until pork reaches 145°F internal temperature.
- [ ] Let pork rest 5 minutes before serving.

### Leftover Ideas
- If extra: Slice leftover pork chops thin for sandwiches with mustard and pickles.
